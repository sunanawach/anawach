Why Nursing? — WA Context

Australia faces a projected shortfall of 70,707 FTE nurses by 2035 (Dept of Health & Aged Care, 2024 National Study). WA has the second-lowest nurse FTE rate in Australia. This makes the Careers Expo the ideal platform for Stanley College to recruit the next generation of Enrolled Nurses.

Event Plan Framework · Part 2

The 5 E's — Visitor Experience Design

The 5 E's map the complete visitor journey from first visual contact through to post-event follow-up, maximising both engagement and lead generation.

01
Entice

Draw visitors in with Stanley College's healthcare branding, bold green signage reading "Become an Enrolled Nurse", and a visually distinctive booth that stands out on the PCEC floor.

Tool: Branded banners + fascia sign ($600)
02
Enter

Open booth front (no barriers), a friendly welcome from a team member, and an uncluttered entry into the Welcome Zone. Visitors should feel comfortable approaching without pressure.

Tool: Open booth layout + welcome zone
03
Engage

A concise 30-second nursing course pitch followed by a hands-on nursing demo (hand hygiene or basic bandaging with low-risk props). Encourages direct conversation with course advisers.

Tool: Demo materials ($200) + staff pitch script
04
Exit

Every visitor leaves with a printed brochure, a HLT54121 course card, and a clear invitation to scan the QR code linking directly to the Stanley College online enquiry form.

Tool: Brochures ($300) + QR enquiry form
05
Extend

Post-expo follow-up within 5 business days: email, phone call, or scheduled course consultation. Stanley College team contacts every QR form submission to convert interest to enrolment.

Tool: EventsAir lead data + follow-up schedule

Risk Management

Challenges & Solutions

Each team member presents one challenge linked to their role, demonstrating that the proposal accounts for real-world event risks with practical responses.

Rinzin — Marketing
High competition from 100+ exhibiting organisations
Solution
Use distinct Stanley College green branding, bold "Become an Enrolled Nurse" signage and an interactive nursing demo to draw attention. A unique activity differentiates the booth from static information displays.
Asmita — Engagement
Visitors may only spend 30–60 seconds at the booth
Solution
A trained 30-second pitch, a quick hands-on demo, and a QR code that captures the visitor's interest even if they walk away — the follow-up email can continue the conversation post-expo.
Annu — Operations
Crowd congestion and poor visitor flow in a busy pavilion
Solution
Open booth front (no barriers), four clearly zoned areas, and unobstructed walkways within the 3×3 m footprint. Follow PCEC ICE principle: clear Ingress, Circulation and Egress at all times.
Annu — Operations
WHS risk during nursing demonstrations
Solution
Low-risk props only — no sharps, no needles. All equipment pre-approved against PCEC's WHS policy. Safe cable management with test-and-tagged leads. Pre-event staff briefing before each day. Emergency Procedures posted in Booth Info Zone.
Anawach — Finance
Unexpected cost overruns or supplier price increases
Solution
A dedicated A$400 contingency reserve built into the budget (approx. 7% of total). Alternative printing and AV hire suppliers identified. Balance payment locked in 4 months before event, protecting against last-minute price changes.
Ugyen — Management
Poor post-expo follow-up reducing ROI on the investment
Solution
All QR code enquiries collected via EventsAir platform (Careers Expo's official registration system). Post-expo follow-up email templates prepared before the event. Every submission contacted within 5 business days of expo close by the Stanley College admissions team.
